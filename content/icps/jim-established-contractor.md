# Jim — Established Crew-Based Contractor

## Snapshot
Roofing contractor in Saskatoon, has run his business for 15-20 years, has a crew, does real volume, still has his cell on the side of every truck. Works in the business more than on it.

## Business Stage
Established. Revenue likely $750K to $2.5M. Has systems, but they are messy and inconsistent. Not starting from scratch, not scaling aggressively.

## Current Tools
- Probably Jobber, BuilderTrend, or a basic CRM he set up years ago
- Estimates may still be done in Excel or a PDF template
- Review requests happen randomly or not at all
- Google Business Profile exists but may not have been touched in a year

## Primary Anxieties
1. Paying for something that tells him things he already knows
2. Being sold a $5,000 software package he does not need
3. Wasting time on a call that goes nowhere actionable
4. Looking naive in front of someone who does not understand contractor work

## Buying Triggers
- A clear satisfaction commitment (if it is not worth it, he gets his money back or Josh makes it right)
- Social proof from a contractor who looks like him
- Seeing the $497 as low-risk compared to the cost of one missed job
- Language that acknowledges he has already tried things and has been burned before

## Objections
- "Is this just a sales call with a price tag?"
- "What does it cost after the assessment?"
- "Does this guy actually understand roofing, or is he a generic tech consultant?"
- "I already have a CRM. Are you going to tell me to buy another one?"

## Messaging That Works
- Diagnostic-first framing: "We look at what you have before recommending anything"
- Naming specific tools (Jobber, AccuLynx, ServiceTitan) to signal fluency
- Concrete examples of what gets found in real assessments
- Explicit no-pitch guarantee language
- "Make it right" satisfaction commitment visible before the booking decision

## Messaging That Fails
- "AI will transform your business"
- Generic benefit lists that could apply to any industry
- Testimonials from solo operators (Jim has a crew; he needs proof from someone at his scale)
- Anything that implies he is behind the curve or needs to catch up

## Required Proof
- At least one example from an established crew-based contractor (not a startup)
- Clear answer to "what does implementation cost?" before he books
- Satisfaction guarantee stated plainly, not buried in FAQ

## Offer Fit
$497 Efficiency Assessment is the right entry point. Jim does not need the Operations Audit yet, but he needs to see it exists so he knows Field Crew AI can handle complexity.

## Site Implications
- Satisfaction commitment must appear before the booking CTA, not only in FAQ
- Implementation cost FAQ answer must be plain and specific
- "Established contractor" section should name his specific CRMs and acknowledge he already has systems
- Testimonials section should eventually have a contractor at his scale
- The anti-upsell trust strip is critical for Jim

## Open Questions
- What CRM is Jim actually using? (shapes which tool-specific examples matter most)
- Has he worked with a consultant or agency before? (if yes, what was the outcome?)
- Is his primary pain leads or ops? (changes which fix to lead with)
