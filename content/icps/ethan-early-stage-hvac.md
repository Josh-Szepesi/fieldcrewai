# Ethan — Early-Stage HVAC Owner

## Snapshot
34 years old. Former HVAC technician, 10 years working for other companies. Went out on his own eight months ago. One helper. Does most work himself. Smart, tech-comfortable, motivated to build something real.

## Business Stage
Early. Revenue under $250K. Eight months in. Getting most work from word of mouth and one Facebook group. Google reviews are thin. Website is basic. No dedicated admin.

## Current Tools
- Jobber or similar (basic setup, not fully configured)
- iPhone Recent Calls as an informal lead list
- No automation turned on
- Google Business Profile exists but is minimal

## Primary Anxieties
1. "I'll pay $497 and be told to buy $5,000 of software I can't afford"
2. Growing too slowly and being out-competed by established operators while still small
3. Building the business on a leaky foundation
4. Solutions that are not DIY-friendly (he has more time than money)
5. The AI label making this seem too expensive or too complicated

## Buying Triggers
- A concrete example of a free or nearly-free fix (proves the assessment is not a software pitch)
- "Done-with-you" language for owners who want to do the work themselves
- Hearing that someone at his stage found real value
- The $497 framing as a one-time cost with no subscription attached
- The satisfaction commitment ("make it right" removes the risk)

## Objections
- "Is $497 worth it when cash is tight?"
- "What are the real costs after? Is there a setup fee?"
- "Is this designed for bigger operations, not someone like me?"
- "Do I need to be technical to maintain this after?"
- "Can I do the implementation myself or will I need to pay someone?"

## Messaging That Works
- "This is still for you" language that explicitly includes solo and early-stage operators
- Naming the specific free fixes (missed-call text-back, review requests, estimate reminders in Jobber)
- "Start with what you already have" framing
- DIY implementation path: "If you have more time than budget, Josh can structure the fix so you can do it yourself"
- Naming Jobber, Google Business Profile specifically
- The stage selector "Just starting out" tab

## Messaging That Fails
- "We work with established operations" (excludes him)
- Anything implying he needs a sales team, admin, or complex CRM first
- Large enterprise CRM names without context (AccuLynx, ServiceTitan feel out of reach)
- AI-first language that implies high cost
- Vague "systems" talk without naming the actual tools

## Required Proof
- One example fix that is free or nearly free (costs nothing, takes 20 minutes)
- A case study or example from a 1-2 person operation
- Clear DIY implementation path stated before the booking CTA

## Offer Fit
$497 Efficiency Assessment. Ethan is the primary ICP for this offer. The Operations Audit is not relevant to him yet.

## Site Implications
- "Just starting out" stage selector tab is essential for Ethan
- The small-operator callout section ("if it is mostly you and one helper") is required
- Anti-upsell trust strip must be visible before the booking CTA
- The "what you leave with" list should include "which fixes are free using tools you already have"
- DIY FAQ answer is important for Ethan
- Testimonial section (when populated) needs at least one small-crew example

## Open Questions
- Is Ethan price-anchoring against DIY research, or against a $1,500+ consultant?
- Does he have Jobber or a different CRM? (shapes which specific example to use)
- What is his biggest current leak: missed calls, estimate follow-up, or reviews?
