# Kathy — Multi-Crew Roofing Operator

## Snapshot
52 years old. Has run her roofing business for 18 years. Three crews, two sales reps, an office admin, part-time bookkeeper. Annual revenue $5.2M, EBITDA around $1.1M. Works on the business, not in it. Sharp, skeptical, and short on time.

## Business Stage
Established and profitable. Wants to either grow to $8M or tighten operations so the business runs without her daily involvement.

## Current Tools
- AccuLynx or similar enterprise roofing CRM (actively used, but not fully utilized)
- Google Ads and Local Services Ads running
- Some reporting, but ROI visibility is unclear
- Review volume has stalled

## Primary Anxieties
1. Getting a generic checklist from someone who does not understand her complexity
2. Spending an hour explaining her business only to hear obvious advice
3. The $497 price signaling that this is designed for beginners
4. Another consultant or agency that oversells and underdelivers
5. 45 minutes not being enough to map a $5M operation

## Buying Triggers
- A case study or example from a multi-crew business at her scale
- Josh naming enterprise CRMs (AccuLynx) without being prompted
- Language that addresses "CRM underutilization" and "ad spend ROI" specifically
- A higher-tier offering that signals he can handle her complexity
- Evidence that he has been in the room with businesses her size

## Objections
- "Is this designed for small operators, not a $5M business?"
- "Has he worked with anyone doing real volume?"
- "Will 45 minutes be enough?"
- "The $497 price makes me think he only works with beginners"
- "I've tried consultants before. They were generic."

## Messaging That Works
- "Pipeline leakage" and "CRM utilization" language (not "cracks")
- Naming AccuLynx, ServiceTitan by name
- The Operations Audit as a credible higher-tier path
- "We audit how leads move from source to booked job" framing
- Acknowledging that she already has systems (she is not starting from zero)
- Examples of what is found in multi-crew assessments (ad attribution, pipeline aging)

## Messaging That Fails
- "Stop letting good jobs fall through the cracks" (sounds like one-truck language)
- "Do you even have a CRM?" (she does, and she uses it)
- "Turn on your missed-call text-back" (she has an admin for this)
- Generic tech consultant language
- $497 prominently featured without a higher-tier option visible

## Required Proof
- At least one example from a multi-crew operation ($1M+ revenue)
- Mention of enterprise-level CRMs (AccuLynx, ServiceTitan)
- A credible Operations Audit path with a scoped engagement, not just a contact email

## Offer Fit
Operations Audit (contact-based scoping). The $497 assessment is a credible entry point for Kathy only if she understands it is a starting point for a more complex engagement, not the full deliverable.

## Site Implications
- Kathy is a secondary ICP. Do not optimize the full homepage around her.
- The Operations Audit tier card must exist and be credible, but should not dominate
- The "Running $2M+" stage tab covers her adequately for now
- Jim and Ethan are the primary targets for hero, problems, and FAQ sections
- Testimonials (when available) should include at least one multi-crew example eventually

## Open Questions
- Has Kathy worked with a consultant who used AccuLynx directly? (shapes credibility signals)
- Is she more interested in growth or operational efficiency?
- Does she have a dedicated marketing coordinator, or is all of that handled by Josh?
