# Homepage Message Map

## Primary ICPs
Jim (established contractor) and Ethan (early-stage operator).

## Secondary ICP
Kathy ($2M+ multi-crew operator). Credible secondary path, not homepage focus.

---

## Hero Section

**Headline:** Stop Letting Good Jobs Fall Through the Cracks.

**Subhead:** Field Crew AI helps contractors map their lead flow, find the operational gaps, and prioritize the simple fixes that reduce missed calls, slow follow-up, stale websites, and lost jobs.

**Primary CTA:** Book the $497 Efficiency Assessment

**Trust strip (anti-upsell):**
"No software pitch. No forced subscription. No 'book this and then buy a $5,000 package.' The assessment starts by finding what you can fix with what you already have: Jobber settings, missed-call texts, estimate reminders, review requests, Google Business Profile cleanup, and simple follow-up habits. Some fixes cost $0. If a paid tool makes sense, you will know the cost before anything is recommended."

**Satisfaction commitment:**
"If you do not leave with at least one clear fix worth more than $497, Josh will make it right."

---

## Problems Section

Audience: Both Jim and Ethan. Written to feel true for crew-based operators and solo operators.

Key problems to hit:
- Missed calls while owner is on site
- Estimate follow-up dies after day five
- Reviews requested randomly or not at all
- Website exists but does not generate or convert leads
- CRM paid for but not fully configured
- No visibility on which lead sources are closing jobs

---

## Assessment Section

**Section headline:** The assessment comes first.

**Tier 1:** $497 Efficiency Assessment — primary offer, for any size
**Tier 2:** Operations Audit — for $2M+ operations, contact to scope

**"You leave with":**
- A ranked fix list: do now, do later, ignore for now
- Which fixes are free using tools you already have
- Which fixes Josh can help implement, only if you want help
- A plain-English next step for missed calls, estimate follow-up, reviews, and lead tracking

**Satisfaction commitment (near Calendly):**
"If you do not leave with at least one clear fix worth more than $497, Josh will make it right."

## Contractor-Specific Callout

**For Jim (established):**
"Already have crews, trucks, calls, and messy systems. You are not starting from zero. The assessment finds what is already there, what is not being used, and what is costing you jobs."

**For Ethan (early-stage):**
"If it is mostly you and one helper, this is still for you. You do not need a full office, a sales team, or a complicated CRM."

## "What an Assessment Can Uncover" Section

Three examples (not testimonials):
1. Small HVAC operator — unused automations already in his software
2. Established roofing contractor — estimate follow-up dying after day five
3. Multi-crew roofing — ad source attribution disappearing after first handoff

---

## FAQ Priorities

Key questions to answer:
1. Is this a sales call? (No)
2. Why $497? (It's real work, not a free consult)
3. Can I implement fixes myself? (Yes, that is part of the point)
4. What does implementation cost? (Specific, honest range)
5. What trades do you work with?
6. What if I don't find it valuable? (Josh makes it right)

---

## Tone Rules

- No em dashes
- No AI-first language in headlines or nav
- Contractor vocabulary: "leads," "estimates," "crew," "job," not "pipeline," "conversion," "funnel"
- Name tools by name: Jobber, ServiceTitan, AccuLynx, Google Business Profile
- Never say "unlock" or "leverage" or "transform"
- Plain language throughout
